01 · Home & landing

Search-first, photo-led.

Keeps the search-first hero as requested. Adds an editorial "what's in season" strip and a proof rail with recent bookings to build live-marketplace feel. Imagery uses the existing portfolio aspect ratios.

02 · Search & discovery

Explore — not just filter.

Keeps the horizontal category rails (they work). Cleans up the filter bar: collapses secondary filters behind one "All filters" drawer so the grid breathes. Map toggle moves to the top-right so it feels like a first-class view.

03 · Artist profile

One page, three questions answered.

The profile already answers "is this good?" (reviews) and "what do they do?" (portfolio). The redesign foregrounds "can I book this now?" — sticky booking card on desktop, sticky bottom bar on mobile, and real availability baked into the hero.

04 · Booking flow

Service → time → checkout → confirm.

Four steps, one visual language. Sidebar summary that updates in-place on desktop; collapsible summary sheet on mobile. Stripe Payment Element embedded with Apple Pay / Google Pay up top — no surprises.

Mobile behaviour

On mobile the four steps share one scrollable surface with a sticky progress bar up top and sticky CTA at bottom — common in booking flows (OpenTable, Fresha). The summary collapses to a tappable line ("$120 · 1hr · Sat 14 Dec") that expands over the page.

05 · Customer dashboard

Your Sparq, at a glance.

Three pillars: next visit (most urgent), saved artists (rebook quickly), get things done (review pending bookings, redeem credit). Cuts cold-start admin panel feel the current dashboard has.

06 · Artist dashboard

Today, then this week.

Artists open the dashboard 10x a day — so it's built for glance-speed, not depth. Today's schedule dominates the view; earnings, new enquiries, and growth nudges sit in secondary columns. Left nav is the existing Studio nav.

07 · Messages

Thread-first, with booking context always visible.

Three-pane desktop (list · thread · booking context). Context panel shows service, time, location, total — so artists can answer "what did I book?" without leaving. Mobile collapses to a two-step list-then-thread flow.

08 · Sign up & onboarding

Two paths. One tone.

Client onboarding is a single step (email + password + location). Artist onboarding is 4 steps — identity, services, availability, payouts — with a hero image rail on the side showing what "done" looks like (finished portfolio). Both live at the same two URLs as today.
